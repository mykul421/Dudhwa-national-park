About the Park

Dudhwa National Park, located in the Terai belt of Uttar Pradesh, is a lush and vibrant forest ecosystem known for its rich biodiversity. From the majestic Bengal tiger to the swamp deer and over 400 species of birds, Dudhwa offers an unforgettable wildlife experience. A part of the Dudhwa Tiger Reserve, it is an important conservation area for endangered species and unique flora and fauna.

Wildlife & Habitat

Dudhwa is home to:

  • Bengal Tigers and Indian Leopards
  • One-horned Rhinoceros
  • Barasingha (Swamp Deer)
  • Elephants, Crocodiles, Sloth Bears
  • Birds: Hornbills, Eagles, Peacocks, Kingfishers

Safari Timings & Booking

Safari Timing:

  • Morning: 7:00 AM – 10:00 AM
  • Evening: 3:00 PM – 6:00 PM

Booking can be done online or at the park gate. Guided jeep safaris and elephant rides are available for deeper exploration of the forest.
