About Dudhwa

A lush Terai sanctuary

Dudhwa National Park is a biodiverse forest in the Terai belt of India. The park protects habitats for tigers, swamp deer, elephants and hundreds of bird species. We promote responsible tourism and conservation.

Guided Safaris

Expert rangers guide jeep & elephant safaris for safe wildlife viewing and photography.

Conservation

Community-led conservation programs for habitat protection and species monitoring.

Birding Trails

Seasonal birdwatching trails with local guides for migratory and resident species.

Wildlife & Safaris

Tiger & Leopard

Large carnivore tracking on guided safaris — sightings vary, but the experience is unforgettable.

Elephants & Herbivores

Spot elephants, swamp deer, and other grazers across the grasslands and riverine habitats.

Birdlife

Over 400 species including migratory visitors — ideal for serious birders and photographers.

Visit & Plan

Best time to visit: November to March. Book guided safaris in advance. Follow park rules and maintain safe distance from wildlife.

  • Morning & evening safaris (pre-book recommended)
  • Local guides & licensed rangers only
  • Respect wildlife — no littering, no loud noises
